WHEAT FARMING GUIDE

Climate Requirements:
Wheat requires a cool, moist climate during the growing season (10-15°C) and a warm, dry climate for ripening (25-30°C). It is primarily a rabi (winter) crop in India.

Sowing Time:
- Ideal sowing time in India: November to December
- Early sowing: October end (for high altitude areas)
- Late sowing: Up to mid-December

Recommended Varieties:
- HD-2967: High yield, disease resistant, 140-145 days maturity
- WH-1105: Suitable for late sowing, rust resistant
- DBW-17: Good for irrigated conditions
- PBW-550: High protein content

Irrigation Schedule:
- Crown Root Initiation (CRI): 20-25 days after sowing
- Tillering stage: 40-45 days after sowing
- Flowering stage: 60-65 days after sowing
- Grain filling stage: 80-85 days after sowing
Total water requirement: 4-6 irrigations depending on soil type

Fertilizer Application:
- Basal dose: 60 kg N, 30 kg P2O5, 30 kg K2O per hectare
- First top dressing: 30 kg N at CRI stage
- Second top dressing: 30 kg N at tillering stage

Harvesting:
- Harvest when moisture content is 14-15%
- Yellowing of leaves and hard grains indicate maturity
- Average yield: 45-50 quintals per hectare under good management
