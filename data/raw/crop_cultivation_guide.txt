**Major Crop Cultivation Guide for Indian Farmers**

**Wheat (Rabi)**
- Sowing: Oct–Dec (North India: Oct 15–Nov 15; Central: Nov 1–Nov 30)
- Harvest: March–April
- Temperature: 10–25°C
- Water: 4–6 irrigations (CRI at 20–25 days, tillering 40–45 days, flowering 60–65 days, grain filling 90–100 days)
- Fertilizers: 120–150 kg N, 60–80 kg P₂O₅, 40–60 kg K₂O per hectare
- Varieties: HD 2967, PBW 343, DBW 187 (for North India)

**Rice (Kharif)**
- Sowing: June–July (nursery); transplanting July–August
- Harvest: Oct–Nov
- Temperature: 20–35°C
- Water: 12–16 irrigations (or standing water in lowlands)
- Fertilizers: 100–120 kg N, 50–60 kg P₂O₅, 40–50 kg K₂O per hectare
- Popular varieties: Pusa Basmati 1121, IR 64, Swarna (MTU 7029), Sahbhagi Dhan (drought-tolerant)

**Maize (Kharif/Rabi)**
- Sowing: June–July (Kharif), Oct–Nov (Rabi in some areas)
- Spacing: 60x20 cm
- Fertilizers: 150–180 kg N, 60–80 kg P₂O₅, 40–60 kg K₂O per hectare
- Hybrids: HQPM 1 (protein-rich), Vivek QPM 9

**Pulses (Gram, Pigeonpea, Moong, Urad)**
- Gram (Chana): Sowing Oct–Nov, harvest Feb–March; needs 1–2 irrigations; resistant to wilt varieties: GNG 1581, RVG 202
- Pigeonpea (Arhar): Sowing June–July; long duration (180–220 days); intercropping with sorghum/soybean; varieties: Pusa 992, BAHAR, ICPL 87119 (extra-short duration 120 days)
- Moong: Summer (March–May) or Kharif; short duration (60–75 days); varieties: SML 668, IPM 2-3, Pusa Vishal

**Cotton (Kharif)**
- Sowing: May–June (North zone), June–July (Central/South)
- Spacing: 90x60 cm (hybrid), 60x30 cm (desi)
- Fertilizers: 120–150 kg N, 60–70 kg P₂O₅, 60–80 kg K₂O per hectare
- Bt cotton hybrids: Ankur 6515, RCH 659, MRC 7351
- Major pests: pink bollworm, whitefly, mealybug
