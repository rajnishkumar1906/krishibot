Drones for Farming (Kheti Mein Drone)

Uses of Drones:

Spraying pesticides/fertilizers – 10-40x faster than manual, saves water & chemical (up to 30%).

Seed dropping (for difficult terrain) – e.g., rice direct seeding in marshy fields.

Crop health monitoring – Multispectral cameras identify stressed plants early.

Soil & field analysis – Create 3D elevation maps for drainage planning.

Government Support:

Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM) – Up to 40-50% subsidy for drone purchase for FPOs/Custom Hiring Centres.

Drone Didi Yojana (2024) – Train women to operate drones for spraying.

Cost & Availability:

Spray drones (10-12 litre capacity) ~₹4-8 lakhs; hiring cost ₹400-500/acre.

Many states have drone hiring centres (e.g., IFFCO Kisan drone service).

Safety Rules:

DGCA registration required (operator remote pilot certificate).

Fly below 120m; maintain line of sight.

Avoid flying over people or during high wind.

Recommended for:

Large farms (>10 acres) for spray.

Orchards and plantations (mango, tea, coffee).

Hilly areas where manual spraying is difficult.

Where to hire?

Government KVK (Krishi Vigyan Kendra) – often have drone demo.

Private agri startups: Thanos Technologies, Marut Drones, Dhaksha (IIT Madras).
