RICE CULTIVATION GUIDE

Climate and Soil:
Paddy (Rice) is a tropical crop requiring temperatures between 20-35°C. It needs standing water throughout its growth period. Suitable soils include clay loam and alluvial soils.

System of Rice Intensification (SRI):
SRI is a method that saves 30-40% water and increases yield by 20-25%. Key principles:
- Use young seedlings (8-12 days old)
- Plant with wider spacing (25x25 cm)
- Keep soil alternately wet and dry (no continuous flooding)
- Use organic manure

Seed Treatment:
- Soak seeds in salt water (10%) to remove empty seeds
- Treat with Trichoderma viride (4g/kg seed) for disease control
- Use Pseudomonas fluorescens (10g/kg seed) for root health

Major Pests:
- Brown Plant Hopper (BPH): Causes hopper burn, yellowish leaves
- Stem Borer: Dead hearts in young plants, white ears in mature
- Leaf Folder: Leaves folded and scraped
Control: Use neem-based pesticides, maintain field hygiene, release natural predators

Fertilizer Management:
- Use Neem-coated urea for 20% better nitrogen efficiency
- Basal: 25 kg N, 30 kg P2O5, 20 kg K2O per hectare
- Apply zinc sulfate (25 kg/ha) for zinc-deficient soils

Harvesting:
- Harvest at 85-90% grain maturity
- Proper threshing and drying to maintain 14% moisture
- Average yield: 55-60 quintals per hectare
