SOIL HEALTH MANAGEMENT

Soil pH:
- Optimal range: 6.5 to 7.5
- Acidic soil (pH < 6.5): Apply lime at 2-3 tons/hectare
- Alkaline soil (pH > 7.5): Apply gypsum or organic matter
- Test soil pH every 2-3 years

Soil Types and Suitable Crops:
- Black soil: Cotton, wheat, gram, sunflower
- Red soil: Groundnut, millets, pulses
- Alluvial soil: Rice, wheat, sugarcane, vegetables
- Laterite soil: Tea, coffee, cashew

Nutrient Management:
- Nitrogen (N): Essential for vegetative growth, apply in split doses
- Phosphorus (P): Promotes root development and flowering
- Potassium (K): Improves disease resistance and quality
- Zinc: Important for rice and maize, apply zinc sulfate if deficient

Organic Matter:
- Maintain 0.5-1% organic carbon
- Add farmyard manure (FYM) at 10-15 tons/hectare annually
- Practice green manuring with sunn hemp or dhaincha
- Use leguminous crops in rotation (Moong, Chana, Pea)

Soil Health Card:
- Provides NPK ratio specific to your field
- Includes micronutrient recommendations
- Updated every 3 years
