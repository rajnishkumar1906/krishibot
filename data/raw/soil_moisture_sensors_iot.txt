IoT Sensors & Soil Moisture Monitoring for Smart Farming

What are IoT Sensors?

Small devices placed in soil that measure moisture, temperature, electrical conductivity (salinity).

Data sent to mobile phone via Bluetooth or SIM card (GSM).

Why Use Soil Moisture Sensors?

Tell you exactly when to irrigate – no guessing.

Save water (up to 40%) – critical for groundwater-depleted areas.

Prevent over‑irrigation that causes root rot or nutrient leaching.

Improve yield by maintaining optimum soil water tension.

Types of Sensors:

Tensiometer (low cost, ₹2000-5000) – Measures soil water tension, works for most soils.

Capacitance sensor (₹5000-15000) – More accurate, gives % moisture.

Time Domain Reflectometry (TDR) – Expensive, used in research.

Affordable Indian Products:

Farmsio (₹3500) – Bluetooth sensor, app gives irrigation schedule.

AquaSpy (₹8000) – Three-depth sensor, works for crops like sugarcane, cotton.

CultYvate – Subscription model (sensor + analytics).

IIT Kharagpur's mKRISHI – Low-cost sensor prototype (available through KVKs).

Installation Tips:

Place at 15-30 cm depth for shallow-rooted crops (wheat, vegetables).

For deep-rooted (sugarcane, fruit trees), place at 30-60 cm.

Avoid spots near trees or compacted areas.

Government Schemes:

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) – subsidy up to 50% for micro‑irrigation + sensors.

Many state horticulture missions also include sensor subsidies.

Return on Investment (ROI):

Sensor cost recouped in 1-2 seasons through water savings and higher yield.

Example: For a 2-acre farm using borewell, saving 30% water reduces electricity cost by ₹3000-5000/year.
