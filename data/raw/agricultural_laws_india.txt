**Key National Agricultural Laws in India (Current & Recent)**

**1. Essential Commodities Act, 1955** (as amended 2020)
- Allows government to regulate production, supply, and distribution of certain commodities (food items, fertilizers, etc.).
- 2020 amendment removed cereals, pulses, oilseeds, onions, potatoes from the list of regulated commodities, except under extraordinary circumstances (war, famine, price spike >100% for horticulture, >50% for non-perishables).

**2. Farmers' Produce Trade and Commerce (Promotion and Facilitation) Act, 2020** (repealed in 2021, but important context)
- Allowed farmers to sell produce outside APMC mandis, anywhere in India.
- No state market fees or taxes on such trades.
- Repealed in 2021 due to protests; now the pre-2020 APMC system is back.

**3. Farmers (Empowerment and Protection) Agreement on Price Assurance and Farm Services Act, 2020** (repealed)
- Allowed contract farming agreements between farmers and agri-business firms.
- Dispute resolution mechanism provided.
- Repealed in 2021.

**4. The Seeds Act, 1966** (being replaced by Seeds Bill, 2019 proposed)
- Regulates quality of seeds sold.
- Central Seed Committee, seed certification.

**5. The Insecticides Act, 1968**
- Regulates import, manufacture, sale, use of insecticides/pesticides.

**6. The Fertilizer (Control) Order, 1985** (under Essential Commodities Act)
- Regulates prices, quality, distribution of fertilizers.

**7. The Warehousing (Development and Regulation) Act, 2007**
- Promotes warehouse development, negotiable warehouse receipts for farmers.

**8. The National Food Security Act, 2013**
- Legal right to subsidized food grains (rice, wheat, coarse grains) for 67% of population.
- Covers farmers as producers and consumers.

**9. The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)** – not a law but a government scheme for crop insurance.

**Current status (as of 2026):** The three farm laws of 2020 have been repealed. APMC mandis are again the primary regulated marketplaces. However, the Essential Commodities Act remains in force with 2020 amendments.
