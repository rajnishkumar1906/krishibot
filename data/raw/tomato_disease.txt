TOMATO DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Early Blight (Alternaria solani):
Symptoms:
- Small, brown spots on older leaves
- Concentric rings (target-like appearance)
- Yellowing around spots
- Defoliation in severe cases

Prevention:
- Maintain plant spacing: 45x60 cm for airflow
- Avoid overhead watering (use drip irrigation)
- Remove infected lower leaves
- Practice crop rotation (avoid solanaceous crops for 2 years)
- Use resistant varieties (like Arka Ahuti, Arka Vikas)

Management:
- Copper-based fungicides: Copper oxychloride (2g/L)
- Mancozeb (2.5g/L) as preventive spray
- Biological control: Trichoderma application
- Apply first spray at flowering stage

Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans):
- Prefers cool, wet conditions
- Water-soaked lesions on leaves
- White fungal growth on lower surface
- Use metalaxyl or cymoxanil-based fungicides

Other Diseases:
- Bacterial wilt: Use resistant rootstocks
- Leaf curl virus: Control whiteflies, use yellow sticky traps
- Blossom end rot: Maintain calcium levels, regular irrigation
