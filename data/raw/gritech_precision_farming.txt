**Precision Farming & Digital Agriculture**

What is Precision Farming?
- Using technology to measure and manage field variability in crops, soil, weather, and inputs.
- Goal: Increase yield, reduce cost, conserve resources.

Technologies Used:
1. GPS/GNSS – For field mapping, auto-steering tractors, yield monitoring.
2. Remote Sensing – Satellite (Sentinel, Landsat) and drone images to monitor crop health (NDVI, soil moisture).
3. Variable Rate Technology (VRT) – Apply seeds, fertilizers, water at different rates across a field based on soil maps.
4. Yield Monitors – Installed on harvesters to create yield maps.

Benefits for Indian Farmers:
- Save fertilizer (20-30%) and water (30-40%).
- Early detection of pest/disease outbreaks via drone multispectral imaging.
- Better planning through weather forecast integration.

Examples in India:
- CropIn (Bangalore) – Farm management software with satellite insights.
- Fasal – IoT sensors for real-time orchard monitoring (temperature, humidity, leaf wetness).
- Ninjacart – Supply chain tech, but also provides quality sensors.

How to Start?
- Small farmers: Use mobile apps (Plantix, CropIn) for AI-based pest diagnosis.
- Progressive farmers: Hire drone service providers for field mapping (cost ~₹500-1000/acre).
- Cooperative groups: Share IoT sensor stations.
